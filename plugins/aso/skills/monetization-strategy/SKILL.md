## Methodology

**Monetization Strategy** — Maximize revenue while maintaining user satisfaction.

### Model Selection

| Model | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|-------|----------|------|------|
| Freemium + Subscription | Productivity, Health, Education | High LTV, predictable MRR | High churn if value unclear |
| Freemium + IAP | Games, Social, Utilities | Low friction to start | Revenue less predictable |
| Paid Upfront | Niche tools, Pro tools | Simple, no churn | Low download volume |
| Free + Ads | Content, Casual games | Scale-friendly | UX tradeoff, low ARPU |
| Hybrid | Established apps | Multiple revenue streams | Complexity |

### Subscription Pricing Architecture

| Tier | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Free (gated) | Core value accessible, premium locked |
| Monthly | Highest price per period, lowers barrier to entry |
| Annual | 40–60% discount vs. monthly total — push this |
| Lifetime | 2–3× annual price, good for power users |
| Family | 1.5–2× individual price |

### Category Benchmarks

| Category | Monthly | Annual |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Health & Fitness | $9.99–$14.99 | $49.99–$79.99 |
| Productivity | $4.99–$9.99 | $29.99–$59.99 |
| Education | $9.99–$19.99 | $49.99–$99.99 |
| Finance | $4.99–$12.99 | $29.99–$79.99 |
| Entertainment | $4.99–$9.99 | $29.99–$59.99 |

### Pricing Psychology

- End prices in .99 (still effective)
- **Anchor with monthly, push annual** — show monthly price prominently, then reveal annual saves 50%
- For expensive subscriptions, display as weekly price ("Less than $1/week")
- Use 3-tier pricing (Good/Better/Best) — middle tier is the conversion target

### Paywall Design

**When to Show:**

| Timing | Conversion Rate | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Post-activation (after aha moment) | 8–15% | Best overall |
| After specific gated feature | 5–10% | Good |
| During onboarding (before value) | 2–5% | Avoid |

**Paywall Structure (top to bottom):**
1. Benefit-driven headline (not "Go Premium")
2. 3–5 key benefits as icons + short descriptions
3. Social proof (rating, user count, or quote)
4. Pricing options — annual highlighted as "Best Value"
5. "Start Free Trial" as primary CTA (full-width button)
6. "Restore Purchases" link
7. Visible close/X button (required by Apple guidelines)
8. Privacy + Terms links at bottom

### Free Trial Strategy

| App Complexity | Trial Length |
|---------------|--------------|
| Simple utility | 3–7 days |
| Health/Productivity | 7–14 days |
| Education/Enterprise | 14–30 days |

**Trial Nurture Sequence:**
- Day 0: Welcome + guide to the most valuable feature
- Day 1: Highlight second most valuable feature
- Day 3: Social proof ("users who do X achieve Y")
- Day 5: Urgency ("X days left in your trial")
- Day 6: Value recap ("Here's what you've done with [App]")
- Day 7 (last day): Clear "Don't lose access" message

### Revenue Optimization Levers

1. **Increase conversion rate** — paywall design, timing, trial length
2. **Increase price** — test 20–30% price increases; many apps are underpriced
3. **Reduce churn** — engagement features, habit formation, pause option
4. **Move users to annual** — better for LTV and churn
5. **Add revenue streams** — IAP, family plan, enterprise tier

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Conversion Rate (free → paid) | 2–10% |
| Trial-to-Paid | 40–60% |
| Monthly Churn | <5% |
| ROAS | >150% |
| Payback Period | <6 months |
